Mani Ratnam is an Indian filmmaker who works predominantly in Tamil cinema. Regarded as one of the greatest Indian filmmakers he is credited with redefining the "range and depth of Tamil cinema". As of 2019, he has directed 28 films, and produced over 15 under his production company Madras Talkies.Mani Ratnam made his directorial debut, without formal training or education in filmmaking, with the Kannada film Pallavi Anu Pallavi (1983).Mani Ratnam is a distinguished filmmaker in Indian cinema, known for his impactful storytelling and thematic depth. Born on June 2, 1956, in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India, Ratnam has left an indelible mark on the industry through his remarkable directorial ventures. His films, spanning across Tamil and Hindi languages, often explore complex human emotions and societal issues with finesse. With iconic works like "Roja" (1992), "Bombay" (1995), and "Dil Se.." (1998), Ratnam demonstrated his adeptness in blending compelling narratives with visually stunning cinematography. His movies are marked by their thought-provoking themes, nuanced character portrayals, and visually striking compositions. Ratnam's influence extends beyond the borders of India, with his films earning international acclaim and recognition.
